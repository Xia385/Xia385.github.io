TCL C6 Washing Machine ​User Interface Design

In our fast-paced modern lives, people seek products that simplify their daily routines and cater to their contemporary lifestyles. TCL C6 series washing machine products are built upon this very idea, harmonizing with the contemporary living environments of users through ​innovative features and ​vibrant interface design.

I​t was developed and designed by a subsidiary of TCL located in Hefei, China. As a member of the project team, my main responsibilities  was to design and implement the user interface, labels and stickers. Through the integration of novel materials and functional visual design, I have improved the usability and distinctiveness of TCL C6 washing machine's UI interface.

Type: Group Client Project

Duration : 9 months (Oct. 2021—Jun.2022)

Tools:  CorelDRAW

Key Skills: User Research, ​UI, UX, ​Prototypes, Material Application

Background

Due to the impact of the pandemic, washing machines with the capability to disinfect and sterilize clothing had garnered the attention of consumers in the Chinese region. According to the 2020 JD.com Laundry Appliance Industry Report, sales of washing machines with antibacterial and mite removal features accounted for 41% of the total, while sales of dryers saw a year-on-year increase of 97%. Consumers were willing to allocate a higher budget to purchase relevant products in order to safeguard the health of themselves and their families.

Nonetheless, TCL washing machines had a relatively small market share, and its research and manufacturing costs were higher compared to competitors. Relying solely on technological innovation made it difficult to distinguish ourselves in the market. As a result, ​our primary focus lay in innovating the washing machine's user interface (UI). Through the visual design of the washing machine's disinfection function and the incorporation of new materials, ​ we aimed to make the C6 series products excel in appearance and user experience compared to competitors while keeping costs within a manageable range to achieve our business objectives.

Challenge

As a large household appliance, washing machines have the characteristics of complex manufacturing processes and craftsmanship. Moreover, there are stringent cost control and quality testing requirements during the product development phase. Therefore, there are two key challenges in the project process:

1. Cross-Team Communication and Collaboration

The development of washing machine UI controls encompasses numerous departments, such as product managers, industrial designers, structural designers, mold designers, electronic control designers, user experience specialists, laboratory testers, and collaborative suppliers. Proficient communication and collaboration play pivotal roles in ensuring the seamless advancement of the project.  

2. Balancing Design Effectiveness with Costs

Striking the perfect equilibrium between constrained manufacturing expenses and an impeccable UI interface is indeed a formidable challenge. It is also where the fascination of decision-making lies. In the forthcoming sections, I will illustrate how to discover this equilibrium across various stages.

Market Research & Trend Analysis

TCL C6 series washing machine fall within the mid to high-end segment of TCL's washing machine product line and are designed for users between the ages of 30 and 40 with families. To ensure that  TCL C6 washing machine aligns with the latest market trends, we carried out an extensive benchmarking process include washing machine aesthetics and interior design trends.

1) ​Washing Mmachine Aesthetics

  • Utilizing display components that match the body color elevates the product's overall aesthetics.
  • Incorporating colorful and concise icons enhances the product's versatility.
  • The brand logo is screen-printed and positioned on the left side of the detergent compartment.

2) ​​Interior Design Trends

  • The minimalist style epitomizes a trend of simplicity and coherence, placing user-centricity at the forefront to enhance practicality. 
  • The utilization of naturalistic colors fosters a serene and comfortable home ambiance.

Competitive Analysis

To acquire a more profound comprehension of the competition within the ​front load washing machine market and to monitor the trends in washing machine program settings, I conducted a thorough analysis of the visual design, panel materials, proportions, and program settings of leading washing machine brands in the Chinese market.

1) SIEMENS 

Pros

  • High level of standardization, ensuring consistent operation panels across products in the same series.
  • Minimal variation in laundry cycles, with no more than 16 options.

Cons

  • A wide range of choices in the function adjustment area, which may not be straightforward to comprehend.
  • Small buttons that are susceptible to errors.

2) SAMSUNG

Pros

  • High level of standardization, 3 series with a total of 3 operation panels.
  • A concise function selection area.

Cons

  • Lack of information causes difficulty in use.
  • Too much text on the second series's user interface.
  • Complex cycles selection make it challenging for users to determine their choices.

3) Midea

Pros

  • A diverse range of panels ​meets diverse user needs.
  • The use of colorful icons enhances the product's appeal.

Cons

  • The colorful images seem disorganized in the last series.
  • In the second series, colorful icons are non-clickable, potentially causing user confusion.

User Analysis

To comprehend users' perplexities and requirements concerning the current washing machine, I engaged in a face-to-face user research initiative organized by a user research group. In total, we conducted interviews with 5 users.

We formulated a set of questions ​regarding usage habits, cycle selection preferences, challenges encountered during ​use, and ​how they resolved them were disccussed with the users.

Based on the findings from the user research, I created two personas to ​describe the pain points and needs of the users.

Problem Identification

Based on the analysis in the last part, the problem can be summaried as following:

  • The industry is increasingly adopting larger interfaces and more vibrant icons, enhancing the appeal of products. Substituting text with icons has been demonstrated to improve information readability.
  • Consumer preferences are increasingly favoring simplicity and user-friendliness in product designs. While consumers appreciate innovation, they prioritize products that are straightforward and user-friendly.

Design Requirement

  • Redesigning the interaction and interface by using colorful icons instead of text.
  • Visualizing the washing and sterilization processes to help users understand what the washing machine is doing.
  • The interface should be easy enough to be understand by the user and will not overload the user visual or cognitive skill.
  • Ensuring a user-friendly operation method to make the washing machine suitable for users of all age groups.

Once the design requirements and user flow were completed, ​we commenced the design of different solutions for these requirements. Over the course of about a month, we conducted three design proposal reviews and feasibility assessments with colleagues from various teams. Subsequently, we reached a consensus on the final design proposal, which is presented below.

UI Kit

It's important to note that all the colorful icons were redesigned to meet the project's requirements. We primarily employed a photographic technique, arranging clothing items neatly at a 45-degree angle to align with the washing programs, ensuring consistent lighting and stacking. This effort led to an overall interface that is both simple and user-friendly.

Prototyping

Prototyping was pivotal in this project, offering an avenue to collect feedback, fine-tune concepts, and ultimately craft products that are both successful and marketable.

For a substantial part of the project's remaining duration, our efforts were dedicated to finding ways to realize our design concepts within the confines of a tight budget. We continuously iterated on physical prototypes, assessed their effectiveness, and conducted practical experiments. Many issues were promptly identified and addressed during this phase, including problems like uneven color display, touch insensitivity, and inadequate brightness.

Final Product

1. Power Off Interface

Display the power button when the device isn't powered on.

2. Power On Interface

All icons light up accompanied by the startup music of the TCL product, after 3 seconds, it transitions to the default interface.

3. Default Interface

The default program is 'Mix', the selected icon is lit up while others are half-lit, the washing time are shown on screen.

4.During the Washing

The screen will show the chosen icon, functions, the remaining washing time. The circular sterilization progress provide information about the  current state.

5. End of Wash Cycle

After the washing is done, the screen will show 'End' and play a 5-second melody. If the door isn't opened within 30 minutes, it will repeat a 10-second melody as a reminder.

Conclusion

During my seven-year tenure at TCL, I've had the opportunity to design various product detail pages with diverse requirements and objectives. I consider the key challenge to be aligning business goals with design objectives effectively. 

In this specific project, I found the initial phase of "Define Problems" to be both enjoyable and pivotal. While drawing upon my past design experience allowed me to establish a preliminary design framework, the task of prioritizing which selling points to emphasize required extensive research and contemplation.

Competitive analysis and user insights provided valuable insights, but for TCL products that didn't inherently hold significant advantages, surpassing competitors purely based on technical merits proved to be a formidable challenge.

In the end, I integrated the three sterilization functions into a unified design to showcase their comprehensive capabilities. Additionally, I employed a distinct visual style, setting us apart from competitors. This approach garnered approval from the review committee, who noted that it could leave a lasting impression on users even after they've left the interface, ensuring they remember TCL washing machines.
