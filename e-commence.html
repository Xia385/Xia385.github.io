All in one Washer Dryer Combo Detail Page

The product detail page is a web/mobile page on an eCommerce site/app that provides the particulars users need to bridge the gap created by online shopping. By designing ​well-balanced washing machine product detail pages, I have increased conversion rates while reducing ​customer service inquiry rates.

Type:  Independent  Project

Duration : 1 month (Dec. 2020)

Tools:  Photoshop

Key Skills: Marketing Strategy,  User Research, Shooting

Background

TCL F1 series washing machine is a front load all-in-one washer dryer combo sold on Chinese e-commerce platforms. Apart from its standard washing capabilities, it also offers drying, disinfection, and odor removal functions for clothing.

As the designer of this product detail page, I was responsible for establishing the product's positioning, conducting user and competitor analysis, determining the design style, and completing the final design.

Challenge

Although Chinese e-commerce industry has made significant advancements, boasting robust logistics and after-sales services, purchasing large home appliances like refrigerators and washing machines online continues to raise several concerns. These concerns are predominantly centered on the following three aspects:

1. Product Image Accuracy

Differences in how product images are displayed on various devices or interfaces can lead to users receiving online purchases that don't match their expectations. This can increase return rates and after-sales inquiries. 

2. Cannot be Experienced Firsthand before Purchasing

Unlike in-store shopping, online washing machine purchases don't allow users to physically operate knobs or buttons, potentially causing misunderstandings during use and more customer service inquiries.

3. Concerns with Functionality and Technical Demonstrations

All-in-one washe dryer combos, unlike simpler products, often feature complex technology to meet user expectations. It's crucial to thoroughly showcase this washing machine's functionality and technical support on the product derail page to alleviate any consumer concerns prior to purchase.

Solution

  1. Use product images that closely resemble the actual appearance of the product.
  2. Place the product in realistic home settings to showcase how it looks when used by consumers.
  3. Use clear and straightforward language to explain the convenience brought by the technology.

1.2 Competitive Analysis

In order to gain insights into the Chinese online market for washer-dryer combo products, I conducted a competitive analysis of TCL in comparison to three key competitors (Haier, Midea, and VIOMI). This analysis predominantly centered on four aspects: marketing strategies, product advantages and drawbacks, design aesthetics, and user feedback.

Summary:

  1. Each competitor has introduced noteworthy innovations in disinfection and sterilization, targeting both clothing and the washing machine drum.
  2. Based on user reviews, the three most common complaints about these washing machines are excessive noise, less-than-ideal spin-drying performance, and relatively complex operation.
  3. VIOMI washing machine has drawn greater attention from younger users due to its emphasis on smart home connectivity.
  4. The three competitors have adopted a similar visual design style that leans towards a tech-focused aesthetic, with minimal representation of their washing machines in home settings.

1.3 User Analysis

To understand people's difficulties with the current ​all in one washer dryer combo and their needs, I participated in a user research organized by the marketing department. Based on the interviews' findings, I created personas that express the main pain points of the users.

2.2 ​Define Design Style

Given that this product falls within the L series and is aimed at a younger users, I chosen a brighter and livelier color palette to enhance its visual distinction compared to competitors. Furthermore, I integrated it with home-style scenes to emphasize its practicality.

Phase 3 Final Dsign

Once I had established a well-defined design framework and fundamental guidelines, I commenced the product photography phase. The handling of shooting tasks by a third-party photography company allowed me to focus solely on providing the necessary product angles, pertinent details, and retouching recommendations. The photography and retouching processes adhered to the following rules:

  1. Given the limited photography budget, I focus on capturing only the necessary angles mandated by the sales platform, such as showcasing the product's front, left side, and right side (JD platform requirements). 
  2. Capture only images that serve a purpose in the design process, ensuring that the total number does not exceed 10.
  3. During the retouching phase, it is crucial to maintain the product's appearance and details as faithfully as possible. This is vital to prevent customer returns or complaints stemming from disparities between the actual product and the images, which could negatively impact the overall performance of our sales channel.
  4. Furthermore, the retouching style must harmonize with the overall aesthetic of TCL washing machine products to maintain consistent brand recognition.

Product Images

Concurrently, throughout the product photography process, I initiated the design of complementary materials and crafted more comprehensive copy. After completing all preparatory tasks, I moved forward with the page design implementation.

Triggers' Page

The ​triggers' page combine the first screen page and ​the pages highlighting the three major sales points.

In crafting the first screen page, I placed the product in common household settings to highlight its practicality. 

Besides, I incorporated the slogan "Steam Sterilization, Rapid Washing & Drying" to address user demands for disinfection and speedy laundry, features emphasized by our competitors. 

Additionally, I unified the three sterilization and disinfection functions to emphasize TCL's dedication and innovation in this area.

Expectations' Page

These pages highlight the washing machine's drying efficiency, ​a feature that users anticipate when purchasing a washer-dryer combo. The design emphasizes not only fast drying but also gentle drying at a low temperature of 60°C to protect garments.

Additionally, recognizing Chinese preferences for cooking styles like hotpot and stir-fry that can leave clothes with lingering odors, addressing this concern is crucial. To illustrate the product's ability to eliminate deep-seated odors from clothes, I incorporated copyrighted images of children in the "Odor Removal" air wash page,  as children often come into contact with their parents.

Lastly, I showcased the inverter and quiet performance to address user concerns about noise levels.

Fundamental Requirements' Page

In the final section of the product details page, I showcased some of the product's fundamental features, including the distinctive TCL drum design, a generous 10 kg capacity to accommodate household laundry demands, an extensive selection of laundry cycles, and thorough product specifications.

The primary objective of this section is to ensure that users, whether they are browsing to the end or making a purchase decision, have access to comprehensive information about the washing machine.

Conclusion

During my seven-year tenure at TCL, I've had the opportunity to design various product detail pages with diverse requirements and objectives. I consider the key challenge to be aligning business goals with design objectives effectively. 

In this specific project, I found the initial phase of "Define Problems" to be both enjoyable and pivotal. While drawing upon my past design experience allowed me to establish a preliminary design framework, the task of prioritizing which selling points to emphasize required extensive research and contemplation.

Competitive analysis and user insights provided valuable insights, but for TCL products that didn't inherently hold significant advantages, surpassing competitors purely based on technical merits proved to be a formidable challenge.

In the end, I integrated the three sterilization functions into a unified design to showcase their comprehensive capabilities. Additionally, I employed a distinct visual style, setting us apart from competitors. This approach garnered approval from the review committee, who noted that it could leave a lasting impression on users even after they've left the interface, ensuring they remember TCL washing machines.
